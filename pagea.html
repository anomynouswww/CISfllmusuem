👑 Queen Idia 👑

A queen… a warrior… a strategist… but wait—how many people actually know her story?

Queen Idia (early 1500s) was no ordinary woman. She was the powerful Queen Mother of the Benin Empire. But here’s the real question… how many mothers help win wars?

She helped her son, Oba Esigie, rise to power—through war, intelligence, medicine, and spiritual knowledge. Sounds like something out of a movie, right?

Because of her influence, she became the first Iyoba (Queen Mother) and even had her own palace. I mean… how iconic is that?

The Legendary Mask

The Queen Idia Mask isn’t just art… it’s power carved into ivory. Think about it—how does something so simple become so legendary?

Created in the Kingdom of Benin, the mask represents strength, leadership, and creativity. At a time when women weren’t expected to lead, she proved everyone wrong. Bold move, right?

Today, these masks are found across the world—in England, Germany, and the United States. Ever wondered how something from Benin became global?

The original mask is currently kept in the British Museum in London. But here’s a question worth thinking about… should it still be there?

Queen Idia may be gone… but her legacy? Still powerful. Still global. Still unforgettable.
